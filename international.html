1) Fulbright-Nehru Master's Fellowships


 Fulbright-Nehru Master's Fellowships is for energetic individuals who demonstrate leadership qualities, have finished a bachelor’s degree and have an experience of at least 3 years. It is designed for outstanding Indians to pursue a master's degree program at select U.S. colleges and universities in the areas of Arts and Culture Management including Environmental Science/Studies; Heritage Conservation and Museum Studies; Urban and Regional Planning; Higher Education Administration; International Legal Studies; Public Administration; Public Health; Women's Studies. The scholarship is provided for one to two years and covers tuition fee as well as living allowance.

Location: USA

2)Edinburgh Napier University Scholarship


 The GREAT India Scholarships by Edinburgh Napier University are a joint scheme between the British Council and UK colleges to help extraordinary and talented students from India in promoting their teaching in the UK and to end up as the representatives of UK for advanced education in India. Chosen students receive the award as a reduction in their overall tuition fee.

Location: Scotland, UK

3)Oxford and Cambridge Society of India (OCSI) Scholarships


 The Oxford and Cambridge Society of India Scholarship invites applications from bachelors, masters and PhD candidates. The goal of the programme is to assist Indian students with pursuing further education at the colleges of Oxford or Cambridge by giving them finance related help. The maximum age eligibility for this grant is 30 years and the reward amount changes every year.

Location: England, UK

 The University of Melbourne welcomes applications for the International PG (Postgraduate) Scholarship programme from Indian students who are interested in pursuing a full-time postgraduate program at their university. It is the highest ranked university of Australia and has collaborated with India to develop a productive relationship between both countries.
